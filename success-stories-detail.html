EU Send The use of the EU Send service enabled DG ENER to setup a system-to-system data exchange solution.

The European Union is committed to building an Energy Union with a forward-looking climate policy.

In this regard, energy efficiency represents a crucial element of the Union’s 2030 Climate and Energy Policy Framework and is key to moderating energy demand. In this context, EU Regulation 2017/1369 requires suppliers (i.e. manufacturers, importers or authorised representatives) to register their products requiring an energy labelling in a European Product Database for Energy Labelling (EPREL) before being placed on the European market. The information to be entered in the EPREL database relates to the energy label, product information and technical documentation. Such information is beneficial for both customers and national market surveillance authorities. In fact, while the former can make better informed choices, contributing to energy savings and to reduced energy bills, the latter can more effectively enforce compliance with the labelling requirements, thereby contributing to a level playing field for industry.

The main activity of EPREL involves the gathering of information coming from suppliers. In this respect, DG ENER had to find a suitable solution that could guarantee the secure and reliable exchange of data with the industry. Particularly, some suppliers needed an integrated system-to-system solution that could ensure the update of this information in a fully automated way. Furthermore, DG ENER had a short timeframe to implement the solution. DG ENER solved these challenges by deploying eDelivery as a managed service via EU Send. The use of the EU Send service enabled DG ENER to setup a system-to-system data exchange solution while outsourcing both the development and operation of the EPREL eDelivery Access Point to DIGIT. This allowed a smooth setup and a simplified implementation process by removing internal administrative complexity. As a result, DG ENER could focus on its policy objectives and deliver an operational database by the required legal deadline. By using the EU Send managed service, DG ENER will ensure the operation of the EPREL eDelivery Access Point and support to the users’ onboarding process.
